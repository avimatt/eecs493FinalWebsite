EECS Help Queue App

By: Avi Mattenson, David Quesada, & Noah Nuechterlein



Background

Our Application, EECS Help Queue. Our goal, to decrease friction in holding office hours across all EECS computing classes. The EECS Help Queue app is designed to be a mobile workstation for instructors conducting office hours in CAEN labs at the University of Michigan. We host digital queues of students that are fully mutable with all requested functionality to go along a with clean slick UI design. A student must only sign up to the queue online and let us do the rest. When he or she becomes the top member of the queue, the instructor will go to his or her workstation and give the student help. Our app provides the instructor with all the tools he or she needs

Features

Login, Choose a class, Submit a Request, View a request, Start and End Visiting, View maps of CAEN Labs, Delete Requests, Begin and End Queue, Request instructor status, Clear, Queue Swipe to Refresh, Stats, and an EasterEgg

Version 2.0

In the future, we look to implement live updates from the web server. We are also looking into do a student side application to make the queue easier to monitor for students. The existence of a administration page for each class is also being looked into, as it would allow a class to have multiple queues, and manage announcements. We are looking forward to implementing these new features in the coming months!




The login page is designed to allow only instructors of EECS classes at the University of Michigan to login. They will then be able to access all queues, but only edit those which they are the instructor for. Instructors must login with their umich account. In the future we plan to hook this up to the Google Identity Toolkit.


On an incorrect login attempt, The user is prompted with a message of their mistake, and the box or boxes with the incorrect information is highlighted.


If the user does not yet have instructor permissions, they can click the text at the bottom of the login page which brings up a templated email to the back end developer who will asses whether to add their permissions to the database.




The instructor must choose a class in order to view it's queue. Classes that they are an instructor for will have editable queues, whereas classes that they are not the instructor for will only allow them to view the queue. At this point they also have the capability to logout.


Once selecting a class, the user can see the queue for students of that class. At this point he has many choices of action. This is classified as the central hub for the user. It is the main page that they will rely on during office hours


In the menu for the queue page, an instructor has the choice of submitting a new request, starting office hours, ending office hours, looking at the map, or clearing all requests from the queue




Should the queue ever become out of date, the user can swipe down on the queue page to aquire the current data set from the server.


By holding down on a specific member of the queue, the user can bring up a dialog where he is given three choices. Start helping the student, finish helping the student, or deleting the request from the queue.


If the instructor chooses to start helping the student, a timer is started in the background to keep track of how long the instructor has been with the student. In addition, the element in the queue is highlighted showing that they are currently being helped, and any other IA should visit the next student on the queue. If the instructor clicked finish helping, both these actions would be reversed.




Upon clicking a specific request in the queue, the instructor will bring up a full view of the request including the issue that the student is having. As a ready option, he can delete the request and go back to the queue. In addition you can see the timer here if the student is being helped currently.


If you bring up the menu in the request page, You have the option to start helping, finish helping, see the map, and delete the request, just as before. Start helping and finish helping will bring up and stop the timer respectivly.


In the case that the instructor is not familiar with the lab and the computer numbers inside of it, the map page shows the layout by computer for any lab the user wants to select. It is helpful for finding students.




When done with office hours and the queue is non-empty, the instructor has the choice of clearing the queue. This deletes all elements, and shows an image in the background of an empty queue.


After finishing office hours, the instructor will press "End Office Hours" and receive the statistics for his session.\n


If the instructor wants to enter his or her own request to the queue in order to send a message to the students, they can do so here. Again, all boxes must be filled out to submit.


If the user presses the submit button 5 times without the submit filling out all the fields, an easter egg appears. The image of the back end developer when clicked starts spinning. On a second click it will go in the other direction.
